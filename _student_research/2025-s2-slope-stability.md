abstract_background: "Deterministic slope stability analyses commonly used in practice fail to account for the inherent variability of soil parameters, leading to designs that may either over- or under-estimate actual failure risk for highway embankments."
abstract_objective: "To quantify the probability of failure for highway embankments using a Monte Carlo simulation framework that incorporates spatial variability of shear strength parameters."
abstract_methodology: "Soil parameter variability is characterised from field and laboratory data using statistical analysis. Random fields are generated using the Karhunen–Loève expansion and embedded in a limit-equilibrium slope stability model. Failure probability is estimated from 10,000 Monte Carlo realisations, and sensitivity analysis identifies the governing parameters."
abstract_contribution: "The study will produce probabilistic reliability curves linking factor-of-safety distributions to input parameter variability, providing a practical decision-support tool for risk-informed design of embankment slopes."
